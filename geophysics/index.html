In Bradford, geophysics addresses the variable ground conditions linked to the Millstone Grit, Coal Measures, and overlying glacial deposits. Our surveys provide critical subsurface data, directly informing foundation design and earthworks in line with BS 5930:2015+A1:2020 and Eurocode 7. For seismic site classification, we employ MASW / VS30 to determine shear wave velocity profiles, while HVSR microtremor survey defines natural resonance frequencies, essential for sites with complex superficial geology.

These investigations are vital for residential developments on sloping terrain, commercial builds on former industrial land, and infrastructure projects where voiding from historical mining is a risk. To resolve ground stability and foundation conditions, we complement seismic methods with electrical resistivity / VES, mapping depth to bedrock and detecting saturated zones or clay variations across the district.

Geophysics in Bradford provides a non-intrusive means of examining subsurface conditions across the region's characteristic Carboniferous strata and overlying glacial deposits. Our approach integrates with a detailed understanding of local geology, where Millstone Grit sandstones interbed with shales and are frequently mantled by variable thicknesses of till, often requiring careful correlation with direct investigation methods such as the Standard Penetration Test (SPT) to calibrate geophysical signatures against known engineering properties. All fieldwork adheres to BS 5930 and relevant Eurocode 7 provisions, ensuring that data collected meets the rigorous demands of local planning authorities and regulatory frameworks.

Methodological rigour is fundamental, employing electrical resistivity tomography, seismic refraction, and ground penetrating radar selected on the basis of target depth and anticipated ground conditions. Resistivity proves particularly effective across Bradford's often clay-rich soils, while seismic methods assist in mapping rockhead topography beneath the drift. The resulting geophysical models are refined through targeted In-Situ, and parameters are validated against laboratory classification tests. UK specification for geophysical surveys follows the guidelines of the Engineering Group of the Geological Society, ensuring a defensible chain of evidence from acquisition through to interpretation.

Typical applications within the Bradford district span pre-construction site appraisal for commercial developments in the city centre, identification of abandoned mine workings and shafts associated with the historic coal measures, and geotechnical assessment for transport infrastructure crossing the variable valley fills. On challenging brownfield sites, the technique is often deployed in conjunction with the Flat Dilatometer Test (DMT) to provide a continuous stiffness profile where conventional borehole spacing would be insufficient to capture lateral variability, thereby reducing overall project uncertainty and enabling more targeted intrusive investigations.

Upon completion, clients receive a comprehensive interpretive report presenting processed geophysical profiles, 2D and 3D models, and a clear geotechnical narrative correlating geophysical units with engineering strata. Deliverables are structured to integrate directly into the ground investigation report, furnishing designers with a robust ground model for foundation analysis and earthworks design. This integrated service transforms geophysical data into actionable engineering insight, reducing the risk of unforeseen ground conditions and optimising the scope of subsequent intrusive works across the Bradford area.
